My motivation for applying to the program

I first encountered programming when I took an undergraduate course in Coding for Psychology at McGill University. Here I learnt the basics of C++ and programs like Gorilla.

My second encounter was in my research at UCL. My study explored factors which influence the emergence of anthropomorphic thinking, namely the difference between observing simple animations and interacting with them. In order to carry out this research I programmed a paradigm which could support both observational and interactional conditions as well as recording movement trajectories throughout. In this process I used the newly developed Bonsai/Bonvision platform as well as SPSS.

I am interested in the Founders and Coders program as it would combine my passion for both problem-solving and creative thought. I am eager to learn new skills and software development would be an excellent addition to my repertoire. Furthermore, I could use experience in coding to inform future Psychological ventures such as creating interfaces for online therapy. Finally, I would use the expertise acquired to create websites for other artists in London, giving them a clearer route to market.
